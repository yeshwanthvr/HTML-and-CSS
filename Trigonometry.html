Trigonometry means "the measurement of right - angled triangles" .

A right angled triangle consists of 3 parts. They are :-
  1. The base
  2. The perpendicular and
  3. The hypotenuse
Since , a right angled triangle has two acute angles , the position of the base and the perpendicular varies in the right angled triangle.

Basics of Trigonometry

There are 6 different trigonometric ratios to learn :-
  1. sine (sin)
  2. cosine (sin)
  3. tangent (tan)
  4. cotangent (cot)
  5. secant (sec)
  6. cosecant (cosec)
According to the picture below , lets discuss the definitions of the trigonometric ratios.


sin C It is defined as the ratio between the perpendicular and the hypotenuse
cos C It is defined as the ratio between the base and the hypotenuse
tan C It is defined as the ratio between the perpendicular and the base
cot C It is defined as the ratio between the base and the perpendicular
sec C It is defined as the ratio between the hypotenuse and the base
cosec C It is defined as the ratio between the hypotenuse and the perpendicular
