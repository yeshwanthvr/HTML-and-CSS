Introducing the Chess pieces





The picture above represents a chess board. In this chess board there are a total of 32 pieces out of which 16 pieces are black pieces and the other 16 pieces are white pieces.

On either side of the board, each piece is repeated at least twice.

There are a total of 6 different pieces on a chess board. So, today, I am here to explain the 6 chess pieces.

1) Pawn



This is the basic piece on the chess board. It is present in the 2nd rank on the white side and the 7th rank on black side.
It has three ways to use a pawn. They are :-

a) It can move 1 or 2 squares forward from its original place. After that, the pawn can only move 1 square forward.
b) I can attack any piece which is in a diagnol to it.
c) It can be promoted to a knight , bishop , rook or a queen if it reaches the last rank that is opposite to it.

2) Knight

The knight is one of the 3 minor pieces on a chess board. It's abilities are :-
It's movement is in the form of an 'L' shape.
It is the only piece on the board which can jump across other pieces without killing them.

3) Bishop

The Bishop is a piece which moves on a diagnol. It's abilities are :-
It only attacks on a diagnol.

4) Rook
The rook is the last of the 3 minor pieces. It's abilities are :-
It only attacks in a straight line.
It can castle with the king to give it some protection.

5) Queen
The queen is the most powerful piece in the board. It's abilities are :-
It can move in a diagnol like the bishop.
It can also move in a straight line like the rook.

6) King
The king is the most important piece on the board. If the king is checkmated, then the game is over. It's abilities are :-
It as a movement range of a square around it.
It can castle for protection against checks.
